Although there are some very cool classes called Geometry in
Construction that build a house while learning geometry, we have
focused on smaller projects tied to the Geometry curriculum at our
school.  Geometry, Design and Construction is offered at all levels
and attracts a variety of students.   We cover our traditional
geometry curriculum, and students use the math they learn to design
and build small and large projects.  We will give an overview of the
course, examples of the mathematics in action and how to choose
projects when you have limited space and budget.

Aimee Hart began her teaching career 29 years ago. She has worked for both public and
private schools in Chicago and at an international school in Rome, Italy. This is her 20 th year
teaching math at New Trier High School. She has served on the board of the Metropolitan Math
Club of Chicago and volunteers as a lead teacher for Math Circles of Chicago.
Devan Castellano is a licensed architect and LEED AP with 25 years of experience in
architecture. For the past seven years, she has taught Architecture and Urban Design,
Engineering and Geometry, and Design and Construction in the Applied Arts department at
New Trier High School, where she emphasizes community collaboration and real world
engagement. Her background includes higher education teaching, research, and sustainability
consulting, and she currently leads dual credit courses at New Trier while operating a small
solo architecture practice.
